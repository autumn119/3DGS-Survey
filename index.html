Abstract

3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has emerged as a transformative paradigm in novel view synthesis and 3D scene representation, achieving real-time rendering speeds while matching or exceeding the quality of neural radiance fields (NeRF). The rapid proliferation of 3DGS variants — spanning dynamic scenes, 4D reconstruction, surface reconstruction, editing, generation, and compression — has created an urgent need for a structured taxonomy and unified evaluation. In this paper, we present a comprehensive survey that (1) proposes a three-axis taxonomy (Task Domain × Module Focus × Technical Approach) to categorize over 100 3DGS methods, (2) establishes a reproducible benchmark protocol across 4 datasets (Blender, Mip-NeRF 360, Tanks & Temples, and a custom Drum Tower cultural heritage dataset) with 6 standardized metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS, FPS, VRAM, Training Time), and (3) releases all code, configuration files, and results as an open-source repository with a permanent Zenodo DOI for long-term archival. Our benchmark reveals key trade-offs between quality, speed, and memory, identifies under-explored areas such as large-scale outdoor scenes, and provides actionable guidelines for future 3DGS research.

Key Contributions

  • 📊
    Three-Axis Taxonomy A structured classification of 100+ 3DGS methods along Task Domain, Module Focus, and Technical Approach, enabling systematic comparison and identifying research gaps.
  • 📈
    Unified Benchmark Protocol Reproducible evaluation across 4 datasets × 6 metrics, with standardized hardware (NVIDIA RTX 4090), software versions, and 5-run averaging for statistical reliability.
  • 🏛
    Cultural Heritage Dataset A new Drum Tower dataset featuring Chinese Dong minority architecture captured under challenging geometry and lighting conditions, serving as a real-world testbed beyond synthetic benchmarks.
  • 💻
    Open-Source Release All taxonomy tables (CSV), benchmark configurations, evaluation scripts, and results are publicly available with a permanent Zenodo DOI (10.5281/zenodo.20263999) for long-term reproducibility.
